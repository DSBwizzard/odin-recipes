Texas Sheet Cake

Description

Texas Sheet Cake is a very rich & moist sheet cake, it’s oozing with chocolatey goodness. This is perfect for serving to a crowd & is quick to put together.

You've probably heard of this amazing dessert, but you may not know what it is exactly. This sheet cake is inspired by the German Sweet Chocolate Cake. A traditional Texas sheet cake calls for buttermilk and pecans. Get ready to create a dessert that will knock your socks off!

Ingredients

Cake

  • 1 13x18 inch cookie sheet or pan
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) butter
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk or 3/4 cup buttermilk substitute (1 cup milk mixed with 1 tablespoon vinegar)
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Frosting

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) butter
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 6 tablespoons milk
  • 3 1/2 cups (16 ounces) powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Steps

Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F.
  2. Spray a 13x18 inch baking sheet with nonstick cooking spray.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the butter, water and cocoa together.
  4. Bring to a boil and remove from heat.
  5. Sift the sugar, flour, salt and baking soda together in a large mixing bowl.
  6. Pour the cocoa mixture over the flour and stir in.
  7. Add the eggs, buttermilk, and vanilla and gently combine.
  8. Pour into the greased pan and place in the middle of the oven. Bake for 15-18 minutes, and prepare frosting while baking. The cake should “bounce” back when touched in the middle.

Frosting

  1. Melt the butter in a saucepan over the stove, or in a large bowl in the microwave. Stir in the cocoa and remove from the heat.
  2. Sift the powdered sugar into the cocoa, and mix in the milk, vanilla and pecans.
  3. Pour over the cake as soon as you remove it from the oven.
  4. Let cool completely before serving.
